What Is Tencel?

Tencel fiber is created from the cellulose of eucalyptus, an evergreen plant which does not require pesticides or irrigation. The fabric is produced through a "closed-loop system," where "99% of the chemicals and solvents used in the process are recovered and recycled, with minimal waste and low emissions.

The fabric itself is soft, strong, and comparable to cotton, but with a more silky texture. Tencel is composed of hydrophilic nanofibrils (very small fibers) which makes the fabric efficient at absorbing moisture. In turn, this creates a breathable, wrinkless material that is perefect for everyday wear.
